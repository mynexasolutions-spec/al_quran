Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a free trial lesson?+
Yes! Every new student is offered one completely free trial lesson — no payment, no commitment required. This allows both the student and teacher to assess compatibility, set learning goals, and experience the teaching style before enrolling.
What ages do you teach?+
We welcome students from age 4 upwards, including adults of all ages. Every lesson is tailored to the student's age, level, and learning pace. Adult learners are very warmly encouraged and well supported throughout their journey.
Do you teach female students?+
Female students are welcome for all online sessions. We maintain full Islamic etiquette in all interactions, and lessons are conducted in a respectful, professional environment. Female students can enroll in any of our courses.
What platform is used for online lessons?+
We use Zoom or Skype — whichever is more convenient for you. A tablet or laptop with a stable internet connection and a working microphone is recommended for the best interactive learning experience.
How many lessons per week are recommended?+
For best results, 3–5 sessions per week are recommended, especially for Hifz and language students. However, even 2 sessions per week yields great progress over time. We fully accommodate your schedule and commitments.
Are the courses available worldwide?+
Yes! All our courses are available 100% online, meaning students from anywhere in the world can enroll and attend live classes. We currently have students from the UK, USA, Canada, UAE, and across Asia and Africa.
What certification do students receive?+
Students who maintain at least 70% attendance and pass the relevant assessment receive an official certificate from Al-Qur'an Global Institute. Hifz students receive a Hifz Certificate, and Qirat students can pursue an Ijazah pathway.
How do I enroll or find out about fees?+
Simply fill out the contact form below, WhatsApp us, or send an email. We'll get back to you within 24 hours with full details about fees, schedules, and how to get started, insha'Allah. The first trial lesson is always free.
