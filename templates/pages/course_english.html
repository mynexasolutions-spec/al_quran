"Learning English opens doors to global knowledge and Islamic wisdom."

— Al-Qur'an Global Institute

Start your journey to speak, read, and write English with confidence through our Three-Month Online Basic English Language Course at Al-Qur'an Global Institute. Designed for beginners of all ages, this program focuses on conversational skills, essential vocabulary, and basic grammar, with an emphasis on Islamic contexts to support your Quranic studies and help you access the wealth of English-language Islamic resources available worldwide.

What You'll Learn

  • Basic Pronunciation: Master English sounds, stress patterns, and natural intonation
  • Conversational Skills: Greetings, introductions, asking questions, and expressing your needs
  • Essential Vocabulary: Topics like family, food, travel, time, and Islamic terms such as Salah and Ramadan
  • Simple Grammar: Sentence structure, present tense verbs, and basic adjectives for everyday use
  • Cultural Insights: English etiquette and Islamic expressions used in English-speaking Muslim communities
  • Access English Islamic resources: Understand Quranic translations, lectures, and Islamic content in English

Why Learn English at Al-Qur'an Global Institute?

English is the world's most widely spoken language — and it's also the primary language of much Islamic scholarship, translation work, and online education. Learning English will help you access a vast world of Islamic knowledge, communicate with Muslim brothers and sisters across the globe, and open countless new opportunities for work and study.

Our course is uniquely designed with Islamic contexts woven throughout, making your learning both practical and spiritually enriching from day one.

Requirements & Certification

  • No prior English knowledge required — open to all absolute beginners of every age
  • Stable internet connection, a device with a microphone, and a notebook for written practice
  • Maintain at least 70% attendance throughout the three-month course
  • Pass a final proficiency test assessing basic speaking, reading, and writing skills
